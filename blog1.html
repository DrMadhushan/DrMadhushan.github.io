Sustainability of online learning after the COVID-19 pandemic in Sri Lanka

Yeah, its great to see you in my blog page. As this is my first blog I thought of write something related to me and my environment. Most of you are following your academic courses online during the COVID-19 pandemic. Some may feel it so convenient to learn from home and some may feel it‚Äôs boring. Of course there might be lots of different opinions on this way of learning (positive and negative). Let me come to the point, I thought to share my opinion about not the present way of learning but the way we are going to learn in the future.

The first thing that in our mind is that COVID cannot stop our day-to-day activities. So, even though it’s not in the actual pace, we keep on moving on our daily work. Of course that‚Äôs a perfect idea/ state of mind. Even from my point of view, our unstoppable working on academics during the pandemic is kind of success. But if we think that we should follow the same way to continue our studies, it‚Äôs not going to be the real story.

As I am a university undergraduate I felt and found some bugs in online learning during the period of learn from home and the following are some highlighted bugs; üòÇ

Barrier to the student-tutor interaction

When we physically present in a classroom or lecture hall, the tutor/ lecturer can sense our state-of-mind without any oral communication. By the term state-of-mind I mean, whether the lecture is boring, students are sleepy, content is not clear etc. Since the online meeting platforms are providing just the verbal and visual communication (including written communication), the human feel cannot be shared among the students and the tutor. So there is a high probability for the content not to be educated properly.

Environment dependent learning experience

In addition to that, the physical and mental environments of teaching and learning are not going to be the same. Thus, the learning experience of the students will not be similar to each other. Of course its likely impossible to make everyone‚Äôs learning environment identical without gathering them to the same place. But we can adapt to this learning method and set up a learner friendly environment around us. A benefit of this is, we can make individually customized learning environment. Is it possible for the entire student population? It is still a question mark.

Practical learning

Learning is not only listening, watching and writing. Practical studies are much important to most of the field of studies like from play-school to engineering, medicine like graduation courses. So the physical presence of students in a lab or workshop is a must. So we can partially follow the course online and do the practical in schools/ universities.

Poverty

This is a key factor to be considered in middle-class developing nations like Sri Lanka when we try to switch to the modern way of learning. Not the entire student population have the same facilities like smart devices, internet connectivity and so on. But if we still want to transform our conventional learning method to the methods like in technically developed countries in the world, we need some time.

Learning is actually a lifestyle and we cannot change it over-night. Yeah! We did it in the lockdown period, but it was temporary and even for that we needed two weeks. Therefore, for a sustainable change, we should have a gradual development in learning style along with the economic development of the nation.
